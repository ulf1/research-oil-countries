**Triangulation logic:** Where all four sources agree on a number, confidence is HIGH. Where ranges diverge, the spread is shown and the likely cause noted (crude vs. total liquids; different reference months; reporting agency). Mistral tends to include gas condensate; ChatGPT reports EIA "total liquids" (includes NGL/biofuels), so headline US/Canada figures are not comparable with crude-only numbers.

### Shadow fleet — divergences and reconciliation

All four sources confirm the shadow fleet is real and institutionalised. Size estimates diverge massively because they use different definitions:

| Source | Figure | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| CSIS (cited by Claude) | 155–591 vessels | Dedicated Russian shadow tankers |
| Mistral / Gemini | ~3,300 / >1,000 | All dark/opaque tankers ever linked to sanctioned cargo |
| S&P Global (cited by Claude) | 3,154 ships | Any tanker lifting sanctioned cargo since Dec 2022 (≈48% of global fleet ≥27k DWT) |
| EU sanctions list (Claude) | 600 vessels sanctioned | Designated by Nov 2025 (19th package) |

**Consensus takeaway:** The narrow-definition fleet (dedicated shadow tankers) is ~600–1,000; the broad definition is 3,000+. Either number confirms that enforcement has become a game of whack-a-mole.

**Price caps (triangulated):** US retained $60/bbl G7 cap; EU+UK cut to **$47.60** effective Sept 3, 2025 (Claude, confirmed by Gemini ~$46). Urals discount to Brent: **~$15–26/bbl** (Claude $25.80; Gemini $15–20; triangulated central: ~$20).

## Where sources strongly agree — key structural themes

1. **Shadow fleet is permanent infrastructure.** All four sources confirm that Russia, Iran, and Venezuela have institutionalised sanctions evasion through opaque tanker networks routing to China, India, and Turkey. Enforcement can raise costs and discounts but has not stopped flows.

2. **OPEC+ 2.2 mb/d cut unwind: April–September 2025.** Timeline and volumes confirmed by all four sources. Pause reinstated November 2025 as Brent fell to ~$60.

3. **US Permian plateau is real.** All four sources agree US crude is near a cyclical peak (~13.6 mb/d) with Permian efficiency gains flattening. Claude and Gemini explicitly forecast a first decline in five years for 2026.

4. **Guyana is the decade's non-OPEC supply story.** All four confirm tenfold growth since 2020 and ONE GUYANA FPSO start. Mistral's production estimate (1.2–1.5 mb/d) is the outlier; 0.75–0.9 mb/d average / 0.9 mb/d peak is better supported.

5. **Atlantic-basin surge reshaping non-OPEC supply.** Brazil pre-salt, Guyana Stabroek, Suriname GranMorgu (2028), Senegal Sangomar, Côte d'Ivoire Baleine all confirmed in at least 2–3 sources.

6. **GCC diversification under fiscal stress.** Vision 2030 (Saudi), We the UAE 2031, Qatar North Field LNG expansion confirmed unanimously. Saudi NEOM suspension and cost blowout confirmed by Claude (unique detail but credible).

7. **Canada TMX diversification.** All sources confirm Trans Mountain changed Canada's export geography. Claude's data: US share collapsed from 93.8% to 51.9% post-TMX; China largest single destination.
