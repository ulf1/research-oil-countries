### The Search & Analysis Prompt

> **"Act as a Senior Global Energy Analyst.** Your task is to provide a comprehensive report on the world’s major oil-producing countries and their primary oil companies for the year **2025/2026**.
>
> **1. Data Retrieval:**
> Use official reported data from the following sources:
> * **Official Agencies:** OPEC (Monthly Oil Market Reports), IEA (Oil Market Reports), IMF (Regional Economic Outlooks), and the US EIA.
> * **Economic Indicators:** National GDP data, export/import balances, and active rig counts.
> * **Activity Tracking:** Recent data on shipping agency tanker flows (e.g., Kpler, Vortexa), refinery construction activity, and upstream exploration projects.
>
> **2. Clustering & Content:**
> Organize the countries into the following specific clusters. For each country, include its **National Oil Company (NOC)** or primary private producers, and its **estimated production (mb/d)**:
>
> * **Cluster A: Sanctioned Countries:** (e.g., Russia, Iran, Venezuela). Focus on 'shadow fleet' shipping data and discounted export routes.
> * **Cluster B: Arab Gulf States (GCC):** (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar, Oman, Bahrain). Detail their spare capacity and 2030 diversification projects.
> * **Cluster C: North America:** (USA, Canada, Mexico). Focus on shale efficiency and pipeline infrastructure.
> * **Cluster D: China:** Focus on domestic production vs. equity oil abroad (Sinopec, CNOOC, PetroChina).
> * **Cluster E: All Other Producers:**
>     * **Major Others:** (e.g., Norway, Brazil, Kazakhstan, Guyana).
>     * **Minor Producers:** Provide an exhaustive list of countries with low oil outputs (e.g., Brunei, Angola, Nigeria, Colombia, Gabon, South Sudan, etc.).
>
> **3. Output Format:**
> Present the data in a **structured table** for each cluster, followed by a brief analysis of how geopolitical tensions are currently shifting supply chains."
